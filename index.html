House rules

How the pool works

1Your squad

Every manager drafts one team of eight riders from the start list before Stage 1.

  • You have a budget of 100 credits. Each rider has a value on the Riders page; your eight picks must total 100 or less.
  • Riders can be owned by more than one manager — pick the squad you believe in.
  • A balanced team usually means one or two leaders plus cheaper stage-hunters and climbers who collect points across the three weeks.

2Locked for the Tour

This is the key rule that keeps it honest:

  • Teams are locked for the entire race — the same eight riders score on all 21 stages.
  • No transfers, no substitutions, no per-stage changes. What you draft is what you ride to Paris.
  • If one of your riders abandons, you simply lose their future points. Choose durable riders.

6Winning & tie-breaks

  • The manager with the most total points after Stage 21 wins the pool and the yellow jersey.
  • Tie-break: most stage wins among owned riders, then the best single-stage points haul.
  • Standings update the moment results are entered — the board you see is always live.

7Prize pool

  • Entry fee: 20 CHF per manager, paid via TWINT to +41 79 410 38 40.
  • Deadline: teams must be submitted and the entry fee transferred before the start of Stage 1 (Saturday 4 July).
  • The pot — 20 CHF × the number of managers — is shared between the top three:
  • Winner — 60% of the pot
  • 2nd place — 30%
  • 3rd place — 10%
